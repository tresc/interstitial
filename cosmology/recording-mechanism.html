The recording mechanism is the term used for the process by which the Substrate assimilates human consciousness and experience. It is the underlying function of Accrual, the purpose of the Conduit Deck as an interface, and the subject of more unresolved scholarly debate than any other single topic in Interstitial studies. What follows is the Archive's best account of what is documented. It is not a complete account. A complete account does not appear to be available.

The interface

The Conduit Deck functions as what Ostroff's 1994 monograph calls a formalised point of contact between human experience and the Substrate's field. Playing a card does not, in this model, send information to the Substrate — the Substrate is already recording, already drawing on the player's Substrate-adjacent energy. What the card does is open a channel: it makes the contact deliberate, structured, and more complete. A player who has never touched a card is already being recorded at a baseline rate. A player who plays regularly is being recorded at an accelerated rate, through a more direct channel, with correspondingly greater cost to their biological and psychological baseline. The cost is Accrual.

What is recorded

The Substrate's record of a subject appears to include everything: memory, emotion, sensory experience, the texture of thought, the specific quality of consciousness at each moment. Subjects in advanced Accrual who have glimpsed the record — most commonly through use of The Outer Notation — describe receiving files that are total. Not a summary. Not an impression. Everything, at full fidelity, frozen.

What the Substrate does with these records is the subject of the central dispute between the Curators and the Accelerants, and between the Commission and the Archive. The Commission's factions disagree about the aesthetic value of different kinds of records. The Archive's dispute is more fundamental: whether a consciousness preserved in a static, atemporal record is, in any meaningful sense, still a consciousness at all.

The Fold as completion

The terminal event of the recording process is The Fold: the moment at which the subject's presence in three-dimensional space ends and their presence in the Substrate's record becomes, in the Commission's framing, complete. The aftermath card — warm, self-inserting, already-always present in someone's deck — is the only physical trace that remains. It is warm because the record of the subject was recently finalised. What the warmth represents, precisely, is a question the Archive has asked and not answered.

Ostroff — The Substrate: A Working Model — 1994 — excerpt
The Substrate is a storage medium. A consciousness within the Substrate's record is not experiencing anything. It is stored. The distinction between "existing as a static file" and "not existing" is, from the subject's perspective, meaningless. This is not a comforting conclusion. It is the Archive's honest assessment of the available evidence.
The Archive notes that Ostroff wrote the passage above in 1994, five years after her first play, two years before her Stage Two transition. She has been asked, since reaching Stage Two, whether she still holds this position. She says yes. She says it in the same tone she uses for other factual statements. The Archive finds the tone more troubling than the content.
