The Substrate and Time

Classification: General Use  ·  Documented since 1974  ·  No consensus model
Substrate relationship to time Outside of it; atemporal
Effect on players Pre-contact anomalies; future information; out-of-order filing
Commission position "Files out of order. Causality not violated."
Archive position Commission position is cosmetic distinction

Because the Substrate exists outside of actualized spacetime, its effects on players do not follow a consistent chronological logic. This is one of the most disorienting and least discussed properties of Interstitial — disorienting because it violates every intuitive framework for cause and effect, and least discussed because the documentation of specific instances is fragmentary, hard to verify, and, in several cases, described by subjects who were themselves in various stages of cognitive and perceptual alteration at the time of the event.

Pre-contact anomalies

Players who have reached Stage Two or beyond are statistically likely, on retrospective interview, to identify an experience from before their first contact with a Conduit Deck that is, in retrospect, consistent with Substrate exposure. The most common is the reflection anomaly — a brief, deniable moment in which their reflection appeared subtly wrong. A second category: a period of inexplicable familiarity, like knowing the layout of a building before entering it, or anticipating the next sentence of a conversation. A third: dreams, in several documented cases, that described specific physical locations the dreamer had not yet visited.

Dr. Thorne described, to his sister, a dream in which he visited an underground room in precise detail before he found it. The Archive has treated this as data. It has not treated it as proof of anything specific, because the thing it would prove is a claim about causality that the Archive has not been able to verify with available tools.

Future information in Stage Three

Stage Three subjects report, with significant consistency, receiving information about their own futures — not as visions or premonitions, but as notifications. In bureaucratic language. Phrased as updates. Several subjects describe the experience as receiving a form letter informing them of a decision that had already been made. They are not told the details of the decision. They are told that it has been processed and that the relevant parties have been informed.

What the relevant parties know that the subjects do not is not documented. The Archive has noted that the Commission's structure — the Face Cards issuing directives to Assessors who manage Players who submit forms to be reviewed — mirrors, in some way the Archive has not been able to articulate precisely, the kind of bureaucratic notification loop these subjects describe experiencing from the Substrate directly.

Decks that depict future events

The Archive holds documentation of three confirmed cases in which a Conduit Deck contained cards depicting events that had not yet occurred at the time the deck was transcribed or copied. In two cases the events depicted were personal to the deck's owner. In one case the event depicted was public and verifiable, and was verified, eighteen months after the card was first documented, when the event occurred.

The Archive has been extremely careful in how it documents these cases. It is not claiming that the deck predicted the future. It is claiming that the deck contained an image of something before that something happened. These are not necessarily the same claim, and the Archive has spent considerable effort thinking about the difference.

The Commission's position

When the Archive raised the temporal anomaly documentation with the Commission in 2001, the Commission's response was: "The Substrate does not violate causality. It files out of order. The distinction is important." The Archive's position is that this distinction is cosmetic — that filing out of order, if the filing is of experienced events, is a description of causality violation with extra steps. The Commission did not respond to this rebuttal. The correspondence is in the file.

Commission internal note — recovered 2008 — provenance: Cincinnati safe house
The Substrate does not experience sequence. It has all of the records. It is always retrieving. What we experience as "before" and "after" are a filing convention, not a property of the record itself. Players who receive future information are not receiving transmissions from the future. They are receiving records that the Substrate has already made of events that have not yet, from the player's perspective, occurred. This is not the same thing as prediction. We are aware that it appears to be the same thing. We have noted the appearance. It is in the file.
The final phrase of the Commission note above — "It is in the file" — is a phrase the Archive uses in its own editorial notes throughout this collection. The Archive encountered it in Commission documentation before it began using it in its own. It does not know if it adopted the phrase from the Commission or arrived at it independently. It has, for obvious reasons, found this difficult to investigate.
