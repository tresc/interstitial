The Empty Mirror

Targeted amnesia  ·  Accrual cost: Moderate
Function Targeted amnesia; spatial unnoticing

THE EMPTY MIRROR

A reflection with no subject.

[imagery shifts in peripheral vision]

Depiction

A standing mirror in a bare room. The frame is plain steel, bolted to the floor with the same unrecognised threading documented in Thorne's description of the filing room table. The mirror reflects the room accurately in every particular, including the angle of light from an unseen window, the grain of the floorboards, the texture of the walls. The mirror does not reflect a viewer. The space where a viewer's reflection would be contains the room behind the viewer, rendered with the same fidelity as everything else — but slightly different. A door that appears in the reflection but not in physical descriptions of the room. A chair occupied in the reflection and unoccupied in front of it. A window through which something that is not sky is visible. These discrepancies are consistent across all known copies.

Mechanical function

The most commonly documented use is targeted amnesia — the card causes a specific person, object, or event to become inaccessible to a target individual's memory. The effect is not erasure. What remains is a negative space: a sense of absence around the missing thing, like a word that will not come. Targets frequently report feeling that they have forgotten something important without being able to identify what. In several documented cases, the target described the sensation as "knowing there is something in the room I can't see."

Secondary uses include: causing a player to become unnoticed by observers (the observer does not forget the player; the player simply does not occur to them); creating a spatial anomaly in which a room appears unoccupied regardless of contents.

Accrual cost

Moderate. Scales with the complexity and recency of the memory targeted. Commission literature notes, without elaboration, that the card "prefers recent grief." The Archive has not found a way to ask what this means that produces a useful answer.

Known appearances

Present in fourteen confirmed decks. Notably absent from three decks whose lineage traces directly to Conduit Deck Zero — an anomaly that has not been explained, as all other expected cards are present in those decks. One of the three deck owners, when informed of the absence, stated they had always known. When shown a photograph of the card, they said they had never seen it before. When shown the photograph again, they said they didn't see anything.
