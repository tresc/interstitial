THE SECOND HORIZON

Two horizon lines. Wrong sky between them.

[shadows cast toward different horizons]

Depiction

A landscape at dusk — or dawn; researchers disagree on the light. The horizon line is where it should be. Behind it, at an impossible distance given the scale, is a second horizon line running parallel to the first. Between the two horizons the sky is a different colour: not dramatically, not impossibly, but wrong in the way a familiar room looks wrong in a dream. Below the second horizon, just barely visible, is the suggestion of a third. The landscape has no figures and no features beyond the horizon lines. There is no visible sun, but two distinct shadow directions are present in the foreground soil, each pointing toward a different horizon.

Mechanical function

Introduces additional depth to an enclosed or bounded space. A room becomes, from the inside, larger than its external dimensions account for. Players have used this to create spaces inaccessible by conventional means — rooms too large to fully search, corridors that extend beyond their apparent endpoint. The additional space does not have a physical source. It is borrowed.

Players report that the borrowed space has a consistent quality across all documented uses: a specific temperature, a specific quality of silence, described uniformly as "like a room just after someone has left it." The Commission's documentation notes that the borrowed space must eventually be returned, but does not specify the mechanism or timeline of return.

Curator doctrinal fragment — provenance uncertain
The second horizon is not further away. It is older. The light between the horizons is not reflected; it was emitted before the first horizon existed and has been traveling since then, looking for somewhere to arrive.
Player game log — 2008
Used the Horizon in the basement. The basement is now at least four times its original volume. Possibly more. I haven't reached the far end yet. I go a little further each day. The floor is consistent throughout. I am keeping notes. I feel fine. I want to note that I feel fine.
The Archive has attempted follow-up contact with the player who submitted the 2008 log. Their Commission cell was decommissioned in 2011. No forwarding information was left. The Archive does not know if they reached the far end of the basement. The Archive does not know if there is a far end.
