THE COLLAPSING STAR

A sun drawing inward toward a perfect black square.

[the square does not radiate]

Depiction

A sun in the process of implosion, rendered in extraordinary geometric detail. The star is not exploding outward but drawing inward, every layer of plasma collapsing toward a perfect black square at its centre. The square has sharp, exact edges. It does not radiate light. It does not radiate anything. Several Players have described extended study of the card's central square as producing a sensation of being observed from a very large distance.

Mechanical function

Accelerates structural decay in non-organic materials. Metal oxidises in seconds. Wood rots. Stone fractures along its natural grain. Glass clouds and crumbles. The effect does not require contact — the card is played, and the target material begins its accelerated deterioration at whatever range the player designates.

Players have used it to corrode locks, collapse frameworks, open passages through walls. Larger applications — structural failure of buildings, degradation of infrastructure — are documented in the Archive's incident files under a classification level the Archive has agreed not to publish. The Commission's position is that large-scale applications are discouraged but not prohibited. The Archive's position is that this is an uncomfortable sentence.

Accrual cost

Moderate to High. Scales with the mass and structural integrity of the target. Larger, more solid, more recently constructed targets carry higher cost.

Commission annotation — undated
The square is not empty. We have asked the Face Cards what it contains. They said: "everything removed from the star." We did not ask follow-up questions.
The Archive asked follow-up questions on behalf of the Commission's annotator. The Face Cards did not respond. The Archive considers the original annotator's decision not to ask, in retrospect, reasonable.
