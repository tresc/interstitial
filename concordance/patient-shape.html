The Patient Shape

Stasis  ·  Accrual cost: Low
Function Introduces radical patience / stasis
Accrual cost Low — see Commission note
Appearances ~50% of all documented decks
Curiosity Most owned, least played card
THE PATIENT SHAPE

A large undefined presence in an open field.

[stars visible; none behind the shape]

Depiction

An open field at night. Flat, empty. In the centre of the field is a shape. It is large — its scale relative to the horizon suggests something the size of a building — but it has no features that can be catalogued. No edges that resolve into lines. No surfaces that reflect or absorb light in any identifiable way. It is present in the image the way that the space between objects is present: defined by what surrounds it. Stars are visible in the sky above the field. No stars are visible in the portion of sky directly behind the shape. The shape is not doing anything. It has the quality of something that has been there for a very long time and expects to be there longer.

Mechanical function

Introduces stasis — a condition of radical patience — into a situation, person, or object. Escalating conflicts pause as all parties develop a sudden, complete willingness to wait. Pursued players become simply not urgent, their presence failing to generate any feeling of necessity in those searching for them. Physical processes slow to an imperceptible pace and become functionally inert. The stasis is not permanent: everything suspended by this card continues, eventually.

Accrual cost

Low. The Commission's note on this card is unique in its documentation: "The Substrate is patient. It recognises the card's function as consistent with its own nature. It does not charge heavily for what it considers a compliment." This is one of two places in Commission documentation where the Substrate's perspective is described in terms suggesting something like preference. The Archive has noted this. It finds it unsettling in a way it has not fully articulated.

Curator doctrinal text — "On the Virtue of the Held Moment" — fragment
The Substrate does not hurry. This is not because it is content. It is because it already has everything in the end, and it knows this, and it is waiting, the way a very large body of water waits for the things that will eventually be dropped into it. The Patient Shape is the Substrate looking back at us. It is not hostile. It is not friendly. It is a depiction of something that has no reason to be in a hurry and wants us to understand this about ourselves.
Player note — handwritten — 2011
I've had this card for three years. I look at it sometimes. Not to play it. Just to look at it. The shape looks different in different light. I don't know if that's true or if I'm just seeing it differently. Sometimes I think it's smaller than it used to be. Sometimes I think that's good.
The Patient Shape is found in approximately half of all documented decks, making it among the most common cards. It is also, statistically, among the least played — a significant number of Players who own it report never playing it and being unable to explain why. The Archive's study found that 67% of Players who own the card describe simply looking at it on a regular basis. 12% described this as a habit they could not account for. The Archive has noted the number and has not decided what it means.
