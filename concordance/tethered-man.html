THE TETHERED MAN

A figure unspooling into black thread, neatly coiled at its feet.

[posture: upright; expression: unavailable]

Depiction

A standing human figure, facing away from the viewer, unspooling from the top of the head downward into an infinite length of black thread. The thread is neatly coiled on the floor around the figure's feet. The figure's posture is upright. The coil is orderly. The figure does not appear distressed. This last detail is noted in nearly every concordance annotation the Archive has received from Players who own this card, always with some variant of the same observation: that the figure's apparent equanimity is the most disturbing element.

Mechanical function

Creates a persistent connection between a player and a target — person, place, or object. The player receives continuous sensory information from the target regardless of physical distance. The information is not transmitted in language or images; Players describe it as a direct perceptual overlay, as though they were partially present at the target's location simultaneously with being present in their own.

The connection does not terminate passively. It must be severed with a Release card. Release cards are not present in all decks. Players who have used The Tethered Man and do not possess a Release card remain connected to their target indefinitely.

Player note — handwritten — Philadelphia safe house — 1998
Mira played the Tethered Man on Thursday. She says she can hear the ocean. We are in Ohio. There is no ocean within reasonable distance. She says it doesn't sound like an ocean. She says it sounds like an ocean the way a word sounds like itself if you say it enough times. I don't know what to do. I am writing this down so that I have written it down.
The Archive has attempted to locate Mira through Commission records. No subject with that name appears in available documentation. The safe house the note was found in was decommissioned in 2002. The Archive has noted the ocean. Ohio does not have one.
