The Conduit Deck is the central artefact of Interstitial — the physical object through which players interact with the Substrate and through which Accrual proceeds. It is a deck of cards. The number of cards it contains is not agreed upon, varies between decks, and varies within the same deck across different counting sessions. The Archive has documented card counts ranging from thirty-one to eighty-nine across its study cohort, with no consistent mean.

The Commission's official position, from a 1987 procedural document: "The deck contains the cards that are present." The Archive has reviewed this statement many times. It has not found it more useful on subsequent readings.

Physical properties

The cards are printed on heavy grey cardstock. No manufacturing process consistent with their production has been identified — they do not match any commercial printing substrate, their edges show no cutting marks, and the ink used for their imagery does not correspond to any known commercial formulation. The imagery itself is executed in a style resembling highly detailed charcoal work. It does not animate when observed directly.

When observed in peripheral vision — when a card is near the edge of the field of view — the images shift. Not dramatically. Not enough to be certain. This effect has been documented by the Archive across seventeen subjects under controlled conditions and has not been explained. The Commission's documentation does not address it. Several Players, when asked about the peripheral animation, say they have never noticed it. Several others say they have noticed it for years and assumed it was normal.

Cards are cold to the touch as a baseline. Cards added to a deck through a Fold event are warm. This distinction is consistent across all documented cases and has become the Archive's primary method of identifying post-Fold additions. See Physical Properties for full documentation.

Mechanical function

Playing a card alters probability and physical reality in the immediate vicinity. The mechanism is not understood. The Commission's doctrinal framing — that each card play is a petition filed with the Substrate — is not a mechanism in the scientific sense. The academic literature's working model, developed by Ostroff in 1994, treats the cards as localised fissures: each play introduces a small impossibility into physical space, and reality's attempt to correct for that impossibility produces the observed effect. See the dedicated article on mechanical function for full discussion.

The count dispute

The indeterminate card count is not a gap in documentation. It is a documented property of the deck. Players counting the same deck on different occasions arrive at different totals. Players counting the same deck simultaneously arrive at different totals. The Archive's most striking dataset in this regard: three researchers counted the same deck independently, in the same room, at the same time. They reported forty-four, fifty-one, and thirty-eight cards respectively. None of them miscounted. See The Count Dispute for full documentation.

The Concordance

The Card Concordance is the Archive's ongoing project to document individual cards across known decks. Entry count is currently nine, with more pending review. Each entry covers depiction, mechanical function, Accrual cost, known appearances across decks, recovered notes from Commission and player sources, and unresolved questions.

The Concordance is not a complete document. It is not intended to be a complete document. It is a record of what the Archive has found. The deck contains more than the Archive has found. The deck may always contain more than the Archive has found.
