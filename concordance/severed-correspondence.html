Accrual cost Moderate–High; old things cost more

THE SEVERED CORRESPONDENCE

Two resealed envelopes divided by a line.

[both opened; both pressed closed again]

Depiction

Two envelopes, identical in size and colour — grey, standard business format — lying side by side. Both are sealed. Both are addressed, but the addresses are not legible. Both have been opened and resealed: the flap of each shows evidence of having been lifted and pressed down again, carefully. A thin line runs between the two envelopes, ruler-straight, from the top of the image to the bottom, passing exactly between them. The line has no apparent source. It is not a fold in the card. Nothing is on either side of the line except the envelopes and the flat surface beneath them.

Mechanical function

Permanent severance of a connection between two entities — persons, objects, places, or concepts. The connection need not be physical. Players have severed a person's attachment to a place, making them functionally unable to return; severed a conceptual link between two ideas in a target's mind; severed an ongoing process from its cause, making an effect continue without traceable origin. The severed connection does not heal. The line in the card's image is, according to Commission doctrine, a depiction of a real structural feature of the Substrate — the record of a removed relationship, preserved permanently as an absence.

Accrual cost

Moderate to High. The Commission's note: "Old things cost more to cut. This is expected."

Black Suits doctrinal fragment — undated
The Substrate does not mourn what it has severed. It records the severing as it records everything else: completely, permanently, and without opinion. We are not the Substrate. We are permitted to mourn. We are encouraged to. The grief of severance is among the most complex emotional states available to a recording. Play accordingly.
Player note — found inside a secondhand Conduit Deck — 2014
I used this card once to solve a problem. The problem was solved. The other thing also happened. I know now that they were the same thing. I am putting this in the deck so the next person finds it. Don't use this card to solve a problem unless you are sure the problem is not also something you love.
The Archive found this note in a Conduit Deck purchased at an estate sale in Columbus, Ohio, in 2014. Columbus is where Clara lives. The Archive does not know if this connection is significant. The Archive has noted it anyway, which is what the Archive does.
