Card Concordance

The Interior Angle

Logical fault in space  ·  Accrual cost: Moderate (deferred)
Function Introduces logical fault into physical space
Accrual cost Moderate; collected at unspecified later interval
Appearances Minority of decks; always found in the middle
Duration Hours to 11 days documented
THE INTERIOR ANGLE

A room whose corners do not sum correctly.

[a figure faces the one true angle]

Depiction

A geometric figure that appears, at first glance, to be a room seen from above — a simple floor plan. Four walls, a door, a window. On closer examination, the angles are wrong: the corners of the room do not sum correctly. The mathematics of the space are internally inconsistent in a way that is immediately perceptible once noticed and which cannot be unnoticed. Players handling the card for the first time frequently experience brief but intense disorientation, described in multiple accounts as "trying to read a word backwards without realising that's what you're doing." A small figure stands in the centre of the room, facing a corner. The corner they face is the one that doesn't add up.

Mechanical function

Introduces a logical fault into a physical space. The affected space becomes navigable in ways it should not be, and un-navigable in ways it should. Exits lead to unexpected places. Rooms loop. Spaces that should be adjacent are not reachable from one another. The effect resolves without apparent residue — duration ranges from hours to, in one documented incident, eleven days.

Players who have used this card report a persistent minor aftereffect: a tendency to pause before entering rooms, as though checking something. When asked what they are checking, they report they are not sure.

Accrual cost

Moderate. The Commission's documentation includes a note unique among Accrual cost entries: "The cost is paid not at time of play but at a subsequent interval. The interval cannot be predicted. The player will know when it is collected." The Archive has asked the Commission to clarify what "the player will know" means in this context. The Commission said the phrase was self-explanatory. The Archive's position is that it is not.

Anonymous addition to Archive entry — editor unknown
The figure in the card is facing the corner because it is the only correct angle in the room. It has found the only true thing and it is staying near it. This is not a warning. This is advice.
The Interior Angle is always found in the middle of the deck, regardless of shuffling. Players who shuffle thoroughly and then locate this card consistently describe its position as "the middle" even when it is not arithmetically the middle. The Archive has documented this across eleven decks. It does not have an explanation. It notes that "the middle" may mean something different to a deck that does not have a fixed count.
