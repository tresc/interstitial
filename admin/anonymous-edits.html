The Archive's editing system requires registration for article edits. This has been the case since 1998. Anonymous edits — edits from unregistered IP addresses or from sessions with no account credentials — should therefore not be possible. Forty-one have occurred. The Archive has documented each one below. Retained edits are those the Archive determined, after review, to be accurate additions to the article in question. The Archive's criteria for this determination has varied by case and is noted per entry.

Entries 010–041 available in physical archive log, vault B. Summary: 20 reverted (accuracy not established); 5 retained (accurate on independent verification); 7 currently under review. Of the 7 under review, 3 describe events that have not yet occurred as of this writing. The Archive is monitoring.

The Archive has attempted to trace the source of anonymous edits through IP logging, session data, and timestamp analysis. Twenty-two edits trace to no identifiable source at all — not an unregistered user, not a known IP range, not a system process. They appear, in the log, as edits made by nothing from nowhere. The Archive's technical staff have reviewed this on five occasions. Their conclusion, filed in 2021: the edits are in the system. They were made. Where they came from is not determinable with available tools. The Archive has filed this conclusion. It has been filing things for thirty years. It has noticed, recently, that the things it files are increasingly about the things it cannot account for. It has noted this. It is in the file.
