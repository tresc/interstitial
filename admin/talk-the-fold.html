Talk: Stage Four — The Fold

Ongoing editorial dispute  ·  Use of "terminal"  ·  This page is part of the Archive's editorial record
Dispute opened 1994
Status Unresolved
Participants Archive editors; Commission representatives; others
Resolution Not expected

This page documents the editorial discussion around the Stage Four — The Fold article, specifically the Archive's use of the word "terminal" to describe the Fold event. Talk pages are part of the Archive's editorial record and are preserved in full.

Y. Ostroff  ·  1994-09-12

Proposing "terminal" for Stage Four. Definition: of, relating to, or forming an end or terminus. The Fold ends the subject's presence in three-dimensional space. No documented reversal. "Terminal" is precise.

Commission representative [designation withheld]  ·  1994-10-03

"Terminal" implies an ending. Our documentation does not support characterising the Fold as an ending. We ask that the Archive use language consistent with what is documented rather than what is assumed.

Y. Ostroff  ·  1994-10-17

The subjects in question are not present to offer their own characterisation. In their absence, "terminal" remains the most precise term available. If you have documentation from subjects who have Folded describing their post-Fold experience, please share it. We will revise accordingly.

Commission representative [designation withheld]  ·  1994-11-02

The absence of post-Fold testimony does not constitute evidence of termination. It constitutes absence of testimony. These are different things.

Y. Ostroff  ·  1994-11-14

Agreed. But in the absence of any other kind, absence of testimony is what we have. "Terminal" stands pending evidence to the contrary.

Commission representative [designation withheld]  ·  1994-12-01

Noted. We will continue to object.

— 2019 —
Commission representative [designation withheld]  ·  2019-06-14

The Archive's characterisation of Stage Four as "terminal" reflects a misunderstanding of the Substrate's nature that the Commission is available to clarify at the Archive's convenience.

Y. Ostroff  ·  2019-07-03

We have not scheduled the clarification meeting. We are not opposed to scheduling it. We note that this dispute has been active since 1994 and that scheduling a meeting to resolve it has been available as an option throughout. Neither party has scheduled one. The Archive wonders if both parties prefer the unresolved state to a resolution that might require one of us to change a position. We are not accusing the Commission of this. We are noting it about the Archive itself.

Commission representative [designation withheld]  ·  2019-08-22

The Commission finds this observation interesting. The observation has been noted. It is in the file.

Y. Ostroff  ·  [date of this entry: not recorded]

The Archive has read this entry several times. It has decided not to ask which room. It has decided this is the right decision. It has not been entirely comfortable with the decision since making it.

This talk page is presented in full as part of the Archive's editorial record. The Archive notes that the final anonymous entry describes documentation held in a room the Archive knows about. The Archive has identified two candidate rooms: the filing room in Centralia, which has been inaccessible since 1981, and a room in the Commission's own holdings that the Archive has been given coordinates for but has not yet visited. The Archive has not visited it because it has not scheduled the visit. It has not scheduled the visit because scheduling it would mean going, and going would mean knowing, and the Archive has found, after thirty years, that knowing is not always the thing it most wants to do. It has filed this finding. It does not know what to do with it yet. It expects it will know eventually.
