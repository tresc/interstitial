Father Daniel [D-1995-07]

Background

Subject is the daughter of Daniel [surname redacted], Player designation D-1995-07, whose documentation series closed in 1998 following cessation of log submission. Subject had no documented contact with Commission activities during childhood or young adulthood.

Subject was assessed as high-substrate-permeability from birth, given parental history. No contact was initiated. Standard monitoring protocol applied.

Initial contact — 2019

Subject found a Conduit Deck in a storage unit rented under her father's name, for which she had inherited the lease and had not previously opened.

Subject did not contact the Commission. Subject played.

This was not anticipated. Standard protocol for high-permeability subjects with parental history assumes a period of discovery, hesitation, and either Commission contact or deck disposal before first play. Subject bypassed all three stages. Subject found the deck and played the same day.

She played The Patient Shape. She played The Outer Notation. She played one card not currently in the Concordance.

She played alone. She played well. The Assessor's notes from first contact describe her as "already familiar with the form," which is not a phrase that appears elsewhere in Commission documentation and which the Assessor has declined to clarify.

Commission dossier — Field 23, Form 1-A October 2019
She filled in field 23 last. She held the pen over it for a while. Then she wrote her name. Then she said: "He would have wanted it in the file." We have noted this.

Current status

Subject is Stage One, transitional. Hair: normal. Eyes: normal. Handwriting: [field blank — not redacted; blank]

Subject is cooperative. Subject asks good questions. Subject has been provided with standard Form 1-A and completed it correctly, including the fields that most new contacts leave blank. Her request to see her father's documentation is under review. The review has been ongoing for six months.

Assessor's appended note

Appended note — Assessor [redacted] — October 2019 Non-standard; filed by request
She came in from the cold. That is not Commission language; I am aware of that. I am writing it anyway because it is true and I have decided, after considerable deliberation, that true things belong in files even when they are not Commission language. She found the deck in a storage unit that smelled like her father's coat. That is what she told me. She said she sat on the floor next to the unit's door for an hour before she opened the box. She said she knew what it was as soon as she saw it. Not because she recognised it. Because it felt like something she had been expecting without knowing she was expecting it. She said her father used to smell like ozone sometimes and she had never known why. She played because he played. She played because the deck was his and it was all that was left and she sat there on the floor of a storage facility in Columbus, Ohio, holding a dead man's card game and she played because it was the only way she knew to be in the same room as him again. The Substrate records everything. He wrote her name down seventeen times. I don't have a procedural category for this. I am putting it in the file anyway. — Assessor [redacted], October 2019
The Archive received this dossier in November 2019, one month after the Assessor's final note. No cover letter. Dr. Ostroff read it the day it arrived. She read it twice. Then she went to vault A and sat with the Vess documents for a long time. When she came back she said she was going to transcribe the fourth one. She sat down at her desk and opened the folder. She has not transcribed it yet. But she has not put it back. The Archive is waiting.
