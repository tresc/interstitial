Re
Your inquiry regarding Document 005
Thank you for your inquiry regarding Document 005. You asked whether we know what happened to Daniel. We do not know everything that happened to Daniel. We know the following: Daniel's game logs were submitted through early 1998 and then stopped. This is not unusual. Players stop submitting logs for many reasons. The Commission does not always inform the Archive when a Player Folds, and the Archive does not always ask. You asked about Clara. Clara is Daniel's daughter. She is, as of the writing of this letter, twenty-three years old. She has not played the game. She has not, to the Archive's knowledge, been contacted by the Commission. She works at a print shop in Columbus, Ohio. She has a gap between her front teeth. She does not know that her father wrote her name down seventeen times on the backs of Commission forms so that it would be on record somewhere that she existed and that he loved her. She does not know about the Archive. You asked why her name appeared in Document 008, in a third handwriting, written by no one we can identify. We don't know. We have a theory. We are not going to put the theory in this letter because we are not certain it is true and we do not want it on record until we are. What we will say is this: the Substrate records everything. Everything that was felt and thought and known. If a person spent the last coherent years of their inner life writing one name down, trying to keep hold of it, trying to make sure it was preserved somewhere — We don't know. We are noting it. It is in the file. Thank you for your inquiry. Please let us know if you have further questions. — Dr. Yael Ostroff Interstitial Studies Archive
Interstitial Studies Archive  ·  Dunmore University Press Typed on Archive typewriter  ·  T, H, E elevated
The Archive has reviewed its inquiry log for early March 2004. No inquiry regarding Document 005 was received in that period or in the preceding six months. This letter is a response to a letter that does not exist in the Archive's holdings. The missing inquiry has been designated Document 009-A and classified as missing. Dr. Ostroff has reviewed this letter and states that she does not remember writing it, but that the argument in the third section is consistent with a theory she has held privately and never committed to paper until, apparently, now. She describes reading the letter as finding a note she left herself in a room she doesn't remember entering. The Archive has filed this description alongside the letter.
