First Contact Letter

Eleven identical copies  ·  Various dates  ·  No return address
Provenance & Classification Commission correspondence
Date
Various (eleven copies, various years)
Condition
Single sheet, folded once. Perfect in all copies.
Provenance
Donated by recipients. No return address on any copy.
Status
Flagged — all eleven copies identical, including "soon".
To
[recipient name varies by copy]
From
The Pale Commission  ·  Office of First Contact
Re
[no subject line]
You are going to find the deck soon. We are writing to let you know that this is expected and that there is no cause for alarm. When you find it, please note the following: The deck is yours. It has always been yours. The fact that you did not know this until now is procedural, not personal. You are not required to play. We want to be clear about this. Playing is a choice and the Commission respects choices. We simply note that most people in your situation do eventually play, and that the situation tends to become easier to understand after the first card. If you have questions, please write them down. Not to send to us — there is no address to send them to. Write them down because writing things down is important. You will want a record of where you started. One more thing: the static you have been hearing. We know. We have known for some time. We want you to know that we know, so you do not feel you have been keeping a secret. You have not been keeping a secret. You have simply not yet been told that other people can also hear it. We look forward to your documentation.
The Pale Commission  ·  Office of First Contact No return address
The Archive has received eleven letters of this type from eleven individuals, all postmarked different years, addressed to different people. The text is identical in all eleven cases, down to the punctuation — including the phrase "you are going to find the deck soon," which in all eleven cases preceded the recipient finding a Conduit Deck by less than two weeks. The Archive does not have an explanation for this. The Archive has noted it.
