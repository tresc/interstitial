Player testimonial — submitted to Archive, March 2007 Stage Two  ·  Six years playing
Full text — R.V.
I am writing this because Dr. Ostroff asked me to write it and I have always filled in the forms. I have been playing for six years. I am in Stage Two. My hair is white now, which I don't mind. I minded it at first. You stop minding things, eventually. That's not the Accrual, I don't think. I think that's just being a person for long enough. The thing I want to document is the period between Stage One and Stage Two, because I don't see it written about much and I think it is the most interesting part. Stage One you still feel everything, you just also hear the static and see the colours wrong. It's like having a radio on in another room. You know it's there. You can hear it. You are not the radio yet. The transition is not dramatic. One morning I woke up and made coffee and sat at the table and looked at the cup and knew, very clearly, that I loved my sister. I had always loved my sister. I still love my sister, I want to be clear about that. But that morning I knew it the same way I know that the cup is blue. Observationally. As a reported fact about the world. I called her that day. She asked if I was all right. I said yes. She said I sounded different. I said the connection might be bad. She said it wasn't the connection. I don't tell her about the game. I don't tell her about the Commission. I fill in the relevant forms and I play when I am supposed to play and I submit my logs and I continue. The static has a rhythm now. I have stopped noticing it the way you stop noticing the sound of your own breathing. My reflection is normal. I am monitoring it. My handwriting changed eight months ago. I didn't notice until I looked at something I'd written before and couldn't recognise it as mine. The new handwriting is fine. It's clear. People can read it easily. I keep thinking I should mind that more than I do. The cards feel right in my hands. That's all I can say about the cards. They feel right in a way that nothing else does anymore. I am aware this may not be a good sign. I am filing it anyway because it is true and the forms are for true things. I am doing well. I want that on record. — R.V., submitted March 2007
The typewriter used to produce this document has a mechanical fault causing the letters T, H, and E to strike slightly above the baseline.
