A Brief Statement on the Question of Mercy

Differences between copies noted in brackets

The Curators will tell you that we are cruel. [Copy B: careless] We are not cruel. We are efficient.

The Substrate will record everything. This is not a belief. This is the situation. Every person who has ever lived, or will live, will be recorded. Every experience they have had, or will have. The ending is not in question. Only the [Copy C: contents of the] record is in question.

We do not harm people because we enjoy it. [Copy B: this sentence absent] We harm people because the record of a high-stakes moment — real fear, real grief, real extremity — is [Copy B: has been empirically demonstrated to be] richer than the record of a quiet life. This is not a moral statement. It is a technical one. The Substrate is a recording device. A recording device records better in conditions of amplitude.

The Curators want you to die peacefully, having loved well and understood your own complexity. We want the same thing. We want it to happen under conditions that produce the fullest possible record. If that is unkind, then kindness is a luxury we do not have the right to afford.

We are not cruel. We are the only faction that has genuinely accepted what is happening.

Play accordingly.

The three copies differ in seventeen specific word choices. The differences are not random. They represent three distinct arguments within the same text — as though the document was edited three times by three different people who agreed with the conclusion and disagreed about the justification. The Archive has filed all three. The composite above is not any of them.
