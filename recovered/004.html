To

From
Assessor Regional Coordination, Central Division
Re
The Voss Matter
Further to our previous correspondence on this subject, we are writing to formally request guidance on how to proceed. As you are aware, Otto Vess — the retired miner in whose home Dr. Thorne resided during the transcription period — presented himself at the Cincinnati cell's administrative address in early September of this year. He is currently eighty-one years old. He is in good health. His hair has been white since approximately 1975, which he attributes to the stress of the period following Thorne's disappearance, and which our medical liaison has assessed as consistent with Stage One Accrual. Mr. Vess states that he never played the game. He states that he never touched the cards. He states that he did, on one occasion, enter Thorne's room without knocking while Thorne was in the middle of a session. He stayed in the doorway for less than a minute before Thorne asked him to leave. He has no memory of what he saw during that minute. He states that he has been hearing static in quiet rooms since February 1974. He came to us because he found our address in Thorne's papers, which he has kept for seventeen years in a cardboard box under his bed. He would like to know, he says, what is happening to him. He would like to know whether he is all right. We have interviewed him twice. He is coherent, cooperative, and precise in his recollections. He has described the static as follows, and we are quoting directly because we feel the phrasing is significant: "It sounds like something being sorted. Like someone going through a very large collection of something, very quickly, looking for a specific one." He has not experienced the reflection delay. His handwriting is his own. We have provided him with temporary accommodations and the standard Form 1-A (Non-Player Contact Protocol). He has filled it out completely and correctly, including the fields that most new contacts leave blank. We are requesting guidance on the following questions: 1. Is Mr. Voss to be considered a Discarded, given that he has not chosen to enter the Commission and has not played, but appears to be experiencing Stage One symptoms through apparent passive exposure? 2. Is his condition likely to progress, and if so, on what timeline? 3. What are we authorised to tell him? We await your response.
Assessor Regional Coordination  ·  Central Division Page 2 missing
Page 2 was not found in the filing cabinet. The filing cabinet contained one other item: a single grey wax seal, broken, bearing a geometric impression consistent with the Centralia slab notation. The seal has been preserved in vault B. Otto Vess died in 2003, aged 93. His handwriting, per a sample provided by his granddaughter, was his own until the end. The Archive never learned what the Face Cards told the Commission to tell him.
