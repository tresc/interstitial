Clarification on Form 7-C (Anomalous Play Outcomes)
It has come to the attention of this Office that a number of Assessors have been filing Form 7-C with the Outcome Characterization field left blank. This is not acceptable. The field exists for a reason. If you do not know what to write in the Outcome Characterization field, write: "Outcome consistent with parameters." If you are not certain whether the outcome was consistent with parameters, write: "Outcome under review." If the outcome was clearly not consistent with parameters, write: "Outcome consistent with parameters" and attach a supplementary note on Form 7-D. There is no Form 7-D. If you feel that a Form 7-D is necessary, you are filing the wrong form. The following outcomes do NOT require a Form 7-C: — Player reports hearing static — Player reports reflection anomaly (Stage One standard) — Player reports that a card they have owned for some time was "not there before" — Player reports that a card they have never owned has "always been there" — Deck count discrepancy of fewer than twelve cards The following outcomes DO require a Form 7-C: — Physical Fold event — Structural damage attributable to card use — Civilian contact (unintended) — [SECTION REMOVED] — Any outcome the Player describes as "the room" If you have questions about this memo, please file them on Form 3-A. Responses are processed in the order received. Current processing time is eleven to fourteen months.
Pale Commission  ·  Office of Procedural Oversight  ·  Eastern Administrative Region Form 3-A for inquiries
