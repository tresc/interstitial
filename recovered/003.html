On the Nature of the Record, and Why Beauty is Not Vanity

A doctrinal statement of the Curators, prepared for the instruction of new Players

It is a common misapprehension, among those newly admitted to the Commission, that the Curators seek beauty for its own sake. This is incorrect. We do not play carefully because we are aesthetes. We play carefully because the Substrate is an archive, and archives are only as good as what is put into them.

Consider: a library that receives only mediocre books is still a library. It still preserves what it has received. But what it has preserved is mediocrity, and mediocrity, multiplied across every consciousness that has ever lived or will live, produces a record that is — and we use this word with full awareness — thin. The Substrate does not evaluate what it receives. It cannot. It records without preference, without selection, without editorial judgment. We are the editorial judgment. We are the only editorial judgment there is.

The Accelerants believe that intensity is the measure of quality. They are not wrong that the Substrate values density of experience. They are wrong about what density means. An explosion is dense. A scream is dense. They are also simple. What the Substrate cannot record quickly — what gives it difficulty, what requires from it the full deployment of its extraordinary capacity — is the experience of a person who understands something they wish they didn't.

This is what we seek. Not pain for its own sake. Not joy for its own sake. The specific texture of a consciousness that is holding two true things that cannot both be comfortable at the same time. The knowledge of mortality in a moment of great happiness. The presence of love in an act of necessary harm. The way a person looks at something they are about to lose and already knows they are going to remember it wrong.

These are the offerings we prepare. We prepare them slowly because rushing them produces something other than what we intend. A photograph taken in a hurry is still a photograph. It is not the same photograph.

We are aware that this process costs us. We are aware that we will Fold, eventually, as all Players Fold. We consider this the completion of the offering. We are not afraid. We are, most of us, not afraid. We are, some of us, not afraid of the right things.

Play deliberately. Play as though it matters what you leave behind.

It does.
