The Red Suits, known internally as the Accelerants, are the more aggressive of the Pale Commission's two factions. Their doctrinal position, stripped to its core: the Substrate will record everything regardless of what the Commission does. The question before humanity is not whether to be recorded but how. A recording of a high-stakes moment — real fear, real grief, real extremity — is richer than a recording of a quiet life. Therefore the Commission's role is to produce the richest possible recordings by engineering the conditions under which they occur.

Doctrine

The Accelerants hold that intensity is the measure of quality. They are not wrong — the Substrate does appear to be drawn to amplitude of experience, based on the available documentation of how Accrual progresses. Their error, in the Curators' analysis, is in conflating intensity with complexity. A scream is intense. A scream is not complex. What the Substrate's recording mechanism most fully engages is not extremity but texture — and the Accelerants' methodology, the Curators argue, consistently produces the former while mistaking it for the latter.

The Accelerants' response to this criticism, from their doctrinal literature: "The Curators want you to die peacefully. So do we. We want it to happen under conditions that produce the fullest possible record. If that is unkind, then kindness is a luxury we do not have the right to afford."

Recruitment pattern

The Archive's study of Red Suit membership has identified a consistent pattern: Accelerants disproportionately recruit from Players who are already in Stage Two and have lost their natural emotional range — those who can only feel through the cards. The Commission's official position is that this correlation has not been conclusively established. The Archive's position is that it has been, and that the Commission's reluctance to acknowledge it is itself data.

Red Suits doctrinal text — composite of three copies — Document 006
We are not cruel. We are the only faction that has genuinely accepted what is happening. Play accordingly.
The Archive notes that the Red Suits' self-description — "the only faction that has accepted what is happening" — is a phrase the Archive has encountered in other contexts within Commission documentation, always applied by one group to itself in contrast to another group. The Commission's Assessors use it about the Players. The Players use it about the Discarded. The Face Cards, in one recovered document, use it about everyone below them. The Archive has observed that an institution where every tier believes it is the only one that has genuinely accepted the situation is either an institution of extraordinary clarity or one that has confused acceptance with position. It has not determined which.
