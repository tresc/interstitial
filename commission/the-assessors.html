The Assessors are the Commission's mid-tier functionaries — the layer between active Players and the Face Cards leadership. They collect and review game documentation, mediate disputes between Players, manage the administrative apparatus of their regional cell network, and serve as the primary point of contact between cells and the Commission's upper structure. In practice, much of what Assessors do is receive forms, review forms, file forms, and generate requests for additional forms.

The cessation of questions

The most consistently noted characteristic of Assessors across all the Archive's documented interactions with them is a specific behavioral shift that appears somewhere in the second or third year of their service: they stop asking questions. Not out of incuriosity. Not out of indifference. Out of what appears to be a settled, administrative certainty that the answers are already filed somewhere and will arrive when they are supposed to. Assessors who have been asked about this describe it variously as "perspective," "patience," and, in one memorable account, "learning to trust the processing time."

The Archive's position: the cessation of questions is either a natural consequence of working in a system where answers are routinely withheld and questions routinely unanswered, or it is something the Substrate produces in subjects at a certain point of Accrual, or it is both of these things, and the Commission's genius — if genius is the word — is in building a structure where the two are indistinguishable.

New contact protocol

Assessors are responsible for managing first contact with new subjects — those who have found a deck independently, those identified through Discarded-tier scouting, and those who, like Clara, find the Commission before the Commission finds them. The Form 1-A (Non-Player Contact Protocol) is administered at this tier. The Archive has reviewed Form 1-A and notes that it contains several fields it cannot account for, including one field that appears to ask for information the subject could not yet know at the point of first contact.

This Assessor has written, to the Archive's knowledge, one thing. It was about Clara. The Archive finds this either significant or coincidental and has not determined which.
