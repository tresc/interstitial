The Black Suits, known internally as the Curators, are the contemplative faction of the Pale Commission. Where the Accelerants seek amplitude, the Curators seek depth. Their doctrinal position: the Substrate is not merely recording but composing — engaged, in some sense beyond full human comprehension, in an aesthetic project — and humanity's role is to be a worthy subject of that project. The Curators play Interstitial with extreme deliberation. A Curator game may last weeks. Each card placement is preceded by preparation. The preparation is itself a kind of play.

What the Curators seek

The Curators' doctrinal literature describes, at length and with evident care, the specific emotional states they attempt to cultivate in play: grief that contains gratitude; love that anticipates its own loss; the specific texture of a person who has understood something they wish they hadn't. They call these states offerings. They believe the Substrate receives them as such. They believe that how humanity is recorded matters, and that the Curators are the only faction that has taken this seriously.

Black Suits doctrinal text — Document 003
What the Substrate cannot record quickly — what gives it difficulty, what requires from it the full deployment of its extraordinary capacity — is the experience of a person who understands something they wish they didn't. This is what we seek. Play deliberately. Play as though it matters what you leave behind. It does.

The Commission's Archive

The Curators maintain the Commission's internal archive — the largest known repository of Conduit Decks and game documentation outside the Interstitial Studies Archive itself. They are the faction most likely to engage, cautiously, with outside researchers, and have been the source of several documents the Archive would not otherwise have obtained. They are also the faction most likely to be encountered in advanced stages of Accrual, having played more, and more carefully, than anyone else. The Archive's researchers who have spent extended time with Curator-affiliated Players describe them as being, in the estimation of one researcher: "indistinguishable from people who are simply very sad in a very organised way."

The Curator position on the Fold

The Curators' position on Stage Four is the most fully articulated of any Commission faction. They describe The Fold as the completion of the offering — the final, total surrender of a consciousness to the record. They consider it the point of the whole endeavour. They approach it, in the accounts of Curator-affiliated Players in advanced Stage Three, with something that is described by witnesses not as resignation or fear but as the specific composure of a person who has been preparing for something for a very long time and can feel that the preparation is nearly finished.

The Archive's founding director, Dr. Ostroff, has described her own position on the Curators as follows: "I understand their argument. I find it coherent. I find it wrong in a way I cannot entirely articulate, which is the most troubling kind of wrong." She has written this down in at least three separate documents and has not revised it. The Archive has filed all three instances. The phrasing is identical in each.
