The Players are the second tier of the Pale Commission and its primary operational body — those who own a Conduit Deck and actively play, risking Accrual in exchange for what the Commission terms negotiation with the Substrate and what the academic literature terms the progressive deterioration of biological and psychological baseline. The distinction between these framings is the central dispute of Interstitial scholarship, and the Players are the population most directly affected by it.

Cell structure

Players operate in cells of three to seven members. Cells meet regularly — frequency varies by cell, but most documented cells meet weekly or fortnightly — to play, to discuss doctrine, and to complete their documentation requirements. Cell membership is stable; Players are not routinely transferred between cells. The relationships formed within cells are, in the Archive's observation, among the more significant social bonds in Players' lives, particularly at Stage Two, when the emotional flattening characteristic of that stage means the cards are the primary available source of felt affect. Playing together is, in this context, not simply a game. The Archive has noted this without knowing exactly what to do with it.

Documentation requirements

Players submit game logs to their regional Assessor after each session. Anomalous outcomes require a Form 7-C. Form 7-C has forty-seven fields. A typical game requires approximately twelve of them. Players are not told which twelve. They fill in all forty-seven.

The Archive has reviewed hundreds of Form 7-C submissions and has noted a consistent pattern: the fields that most Players leave blank on first submission are never the same fields. There is no universal unused field. The Commission's documentation of which fields apply to which situations is either extremely nuanced or entirely absent, and the Archive has not been able to determine which.

Recovered document — player testimonial, 2007 — Document 002
I fill in the relevant forms and I play when I am supposed to play and I submit my logs and I continue. The static has a rhythm now. I have stopped noticing it the way you stop noticing the sound of your own breathing.
The Archive's study of Form 7-C completion patterns has revealed one consistent anomaly: Field 23, the Player Name field, is the field most frequently left blank by first-time submitters and most consistently completed — with unusual deliberateness, per Assessor annotations — by experienced Players. The Archive does not know what to make of this. It has filed it. It thinks about it sometimes.
