The organisation that formed around Interstitial following the emergence of the Conduit Deck into underground circulation is known as the Pale Commission. The name's origin is debated: the most credible theory holds that it derives from an early internal document referring to "the pale margin between what is and what was removed" — pale here in the sense of a boundary, a fence, a line beyond which one is outside. The Commission, in its own estimation, administers that line.

The Pale Commission does not describe itself as a religion. It describes itself, in its own recovered literature, variously as "a body of procedural oversight," "an administrative organ of the Accord," and, in one striking phrase from an undated internal memo: "the only institution that has correctly identified the nature of the situation." It functions, structurally, like a regulatory agency staffed by people who believe they are the only thing standing between humanity and its own paperwork.

Structure

The Commission operates through a four-tier hierarchy and a distributed cell structure with no known central headquarters. Communication between cells passes through regional Assessors. Policy and doctrine issue from the Face Cards — the Commission's leadership tier — by routes the Archive has not been able to fully map.

Factions

The Commission is formally unified but contains two deeply opposed philosophical factions that have not produced an open schism — in part because both factions believe open schism would be procedurally incorrect. The Red Suits (the Accelerants) hold that the Substrate values density of experience and that the Commission's role is to maximise it. The Black Suits (the Curators) hold that the Substrate values complexity and that the Commission's role is to cultivate it. Both factions play Interstitial. Both are progressing through Accrual. They disagree about what this means and agree that the disagreement is, in the long run, irrelevant.

Relationship with the Archive

The Commission's relationship with the Interstitial Studies Archive is the subject of its own extensive documentation. The short version: the Commission cooperates selectively, withholds more than it shares, has misrepresented Archive research in its own materials, has provided the Archive with documents it could not have obtained otherwise, and has, on at least three occasions, apparently known things about the Archive's researchers before the Archive knew them. Dr. Ostroff's summary, from a 2008 lecture: "They are not our partners. They are not our adversaries. They are something we don't have a good word for, and they know it, and they find this useful."

Commission response to Archive inquiry — 1995 Re: Why a card game
The game was not chosen. The game is the only form in which the negotiation could be made legible to both parties. Cards have suits. Suits have order. Order has rules. Rules create the margin between what is permitted and what is not. The Substrate exists in that margin. The game does not describe the negotiation. The game is the negotiation. We trust this clarifies your inquiry.
The Archive's editors noted, in the margin of the archived copy of this letter, in pencil, in handwriting that does not belong to any current or former staff member: "It does." The annotation has been there since the letter arrived. No one recalls writing it. The Archive has filed it as Document 013, pending classification.
