The Face Cards are the leadership tier of the Pale Commission. Their number is never officially confirmed — different Commission documents list different totals, ranging from three to six, which may reflect genuine turnover or may reflect something else. They do not play Interstitial. They have not played in years, or decades. Their bodies have undergone extensive Accrual. They are, in the Commission's framing, the closest living approximation of what full integration with the Substrate produces in a human body while that body remains present in three-dimensional space.

Physical condition

All documented Face Cards are in advanced Stage Three. Dermal patterning is extensive — the Commission's own medical documentation describes coverage of 60% or more of body surface area in several cases. Pigmentation is absent. Sleep and nutritional requirements have ceased. The reflection delay, measured in the one Face Card subject the Archive has been able to document directly, stood at 1.1 seconds — at the upper end of the Stage Three range.

Commission medical assessment — recovered 2003 — Philadelphia safe house
Subject presents with the expected geometric dermal patterning across approximately 60% of body surface area. Pigmentation fully absent. Reflexes normal by modified scale. Subject reports no sleep requirement since Q3 of the previous fiscal year. Emotional range: functional. Subject was asked whether they were well. Subject said yes. Subject's answer registered at an unusual frequency. Noted for file.

Summations

The Face Cards periodically perform what the Commission calls a Summation — a formal game of Interstitial played not against an opponent but against the room itself. No one who has witnessed a Summation has provided a consistent account of what occurs. The Archive has collected fourteen witness testimonies from across its source network. The testimonies agree that something happened, that it involved at least one card being played, and that the atmosphere of the room changed in a way witnesses consistently describe but cannot make consistent with one another. The Archive has filed all fourteen accounts. They do not fit together. They are filed anyway.

The count discrepancy

That the number of Face Cards is never definitively confirmed — and that different documents list different numbers — is a feature the Archive has spent time examining. The most mundane explanation is administrative: turnover is not publicised and documents are not updated. A less mundane explanation is that the count varies for the same reason the Conduit Deck's card count varies: the Face Cards, sufficiently integrated with the Substrate, have acquired some of the deck's indeterminate properties. The Archive treats this as a hypothesis, not a conclusion. It has noted it. It hopes the mundane explanation is correct.

The Archive has made six formal requests, over its history, for a meeting with a Face Card representative. Three requests received no response. Two received responses stating that a meeting was not currently procedurally available but would be noted for future scheduling. One received a response that the Archive's meeting request had been received and reviewed and that the Archive's questions had already been answered in documents it had not yet found. The Archive has been looking for those documents since 2009. It has not found them. It has not stopped looking.
